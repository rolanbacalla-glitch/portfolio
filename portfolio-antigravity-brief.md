# Portfolio Homepage Redesign Brief for Antigravity

> Note: I’m British, so please use UK English spelling and grammar consistently (e.g. “optimise”, “favourite”, “colour”, “behaviour”). Avoid US spellings.  

## Overview

Redesign and rewrite my portfolio homepage so it keeps its premium, modern, motion-rich feel, but becomes much clearer, more credible, and more effective for hiring managers and freelance clients.

This site is for **Rolan Bacalla**, a UK-based UX/UI designer with strengths in product design, UX research, interaction design, usability testing, and design systems. The current homepage looks polished, but the messaging is too abstract and makes the site feel more like a creative-developer concept template than a real portfolio. A strong design portfolio homepage should immediately communicate name, role, value, and clear paths to case studies and contact.  

## Goals

- Keep the high-end visual and motion quality, but improve clarity and credibility.  
- Clarify who I am and what I do above the fold.  
- Make the work feel real and case-study-driven, not speculative or fictional.  
- Add stronger trust and credibility signals near the top of the page.  
- Rewrite copy so it sounds premium, human, and grounded, not vague or overly theatrical.  
- Improve CTA clarity, contact credibility, focus states, and accessible link/button text.  

## Audience

1. **Hiring managers and recruiters** scanning many portfolios quickly.  
2. **Freelance clients** looking for a capable product designer to help with digital products.  

The homepage must help them understand within seconds:

- My name  
- My role  
- My areas of expertise  
- What kind of work I do  
- Where to click next  
- How to contact me

## Navigation & structure

### Requirements

- Clear top navigation with items such as: Work, About, Expertise, Contact, and CV (if available).  
- One clear `<h1>` on the page, then logical heading levels for each section.  
- Sections should be easy to scan: short headings, concise copy, good spacing.  
- Do not bury work under long intros; hiring managers often scan quickly and prioritise case studies.  

---

## Accessibility & UX

### Requirements

- Use semantic HTML and a logical heading hierarchy.  
- Provide visible keyboard focus states for navigation, buttons, cards, and social links.  
- Use descriptive button and link text (e.g. “View case study”, “Email Rolan”), not vague labels like “Learn more”.  
- Maintain good colour contrast and comfortable body text size.  
- Ensure mobile layouts are clean, with generous spacing and tappable targets.  
- Respect UK spelling and grammar throughout (e.g. “optimise”, “organisation”, “focussed” or “focused” used consistently).
